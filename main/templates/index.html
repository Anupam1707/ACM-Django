Upcoming Events

ACM-W Launch

The ACM Student Chapter will officially launch its ACM-W initiative to promote women in the field of computing. The launch event will include an inaugural talk by a guest speaker, short sessions on diversity and inclusion in technology, and an open-floor discussion on how ACM-W can create opportunities for female students. It will also feature interactive icebreakers and networking activities to encourage participation and build a strong community.

September 23, 2025

Escape Room (Code Edition)

A gamified technical escape room will be built around coding puzzles, riddles, and logical challenges. Teams will be shuffled, and roles such as Hacker, Detective, or Debugger will be randomly assigned to participants, giving each member unique strengths during the event. Special twists will include lifelines (like time freeze or hint drop), bonus puzzles for extra points, and hidden agents with secret powers. The format will ensure teamwork, creativity, and problem-solving skills are tested in a highly engaging way.

October 27, 2025

ACM Summit 2025

The flagship multi-day event of the year, ACM Summit 2025, will bring together students, professionals, and alumni. The summit will include keynote speeches by industry experts, panel discussions on emerging fields like AI, cybersecurity, and cloud computing, hackathons with real-world problem statements, and showcases of student-led projects. With a mix of competition, learning, and networking, the summit will become a milestone event, establishing ACM as a hub of innovation and collaboration.

December 19–21, 2025

Hack-a-Product (Mini Startup Sprint)

A unique innovation sprint will allow students to work in small, cross-disciplinary teams to create product prototypes addressing real-world challenges. Over the day, teams will brainstorm ideas, sketch wireframes, build quick models, and pitch their solutions as if launching a startup. Mentorship sessions will help participants refine their business logic and technical aspects, while judges will evaluate creativity, practicality, and market relevance. It will give students a real taste of entrepreneurial thinking in a compact format.

January 6, 2026

Innovation Challenge (Theme-Based)

A theme-based innovation competition will allow participants to tackle problems aligned with societal, industrial, or futuristic challenges. Teams will propose solutions ranging from software applications to creative hardware prototypes. Judges will evaluate not only technical depth but also innovation, feasibility, and presentation. The event will encourage fresh thinking, creativity under constraints, and teamwork, giving students an open platform to experiment and showcase their unique problem-solving abilities.

January 13, 2026

Debate/Panel Showdown (Inter-College)

A two-day inter-college event will combine debates, panels, and cultural interaction.
Day 1 (Jan 20): Technical debate rounds will be held on trending tech topics, non-technical debates for fun, and a panel discussion with experts where the audience will actively participate. Fun games such as Two Truths and a Lie will also be included for engagement.
Day 2 (Jan 21): Final rounds of debates and panel sessions will follow, along with cultural performances like music and dance. The event will conclude with an awards ceremony recognizing the best debaters, most thoughtful arguments, and audience favorites. It will be both intellectually stimulating and socially vibrant.

January 20–21, 2026

3-Day Webinar & Activities

A three-day activity series will be designed in a style similar to Model UN, blending technical and fun challenges.
Day 1 (Jan 23): The day will open with a Tech Talk from an invited expert, followed by icebreaker games and team formation, where participants will be shuffled into new groups and briefed on the tasks for the next day.
Day 2 (Jan 24): The main technical competition will feature either an Escape Room, Hackathon, or code-related games, where each team member will play a unique role to solve problems.
Day 3 (Jan 25): A relaxation and feedback day will include casual offline games, storytelling, and sharing experiences, ensuring participants end the series with both memories and learning.

January 23–25, 2026

CodeSwap Challenge

A highly interactive coding competition will test debugging and collaboration skills. Teams will be given a small coding task to begin with, and after 30 minutes, will swap their incomplete code with another team. They will then have 3–4 hours to debug, optimize, and enhance the received code without knowing the original intent. Finally, each team will present its improved version, and awards will be given in categories like Best Debugged Code, Most Creative Solution, and Best Collaboration. The unique format will emphasize adaptability, communication, and problem-solving under unusual conditions.
